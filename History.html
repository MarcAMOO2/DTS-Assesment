History Of Games

Video Games were first invented in 1950s and 1960s when computer scientists started developing simple games and simulations on a mainframe computer in 1962 as one of the first such games to be played with a video display.

The early 1970s brought the first consumer-ready video game hardware: the first home video game console, the Magnavox Odyssey, and the first arcade video games, Space and Pong, the latter which was later made into a home console version.

Due to this, multiple companies came up to try and capture Pong's success in both the arcade and the home by creating many clones of the game, this caused a series of boom and bust cycles due to "oversaturation and lack of innovation".
